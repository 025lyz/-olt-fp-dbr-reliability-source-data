Title
TMM-validated resonance correction for inverse design of mid-infrared Ge/SiO2 FP-DBR filters

Abstract
Inverse design of resonant multilayer optical filters requires criteria beyond global spectral mean-squared error. A candidate spectrum can have a small global error while missing a resonance, introducing a false passband, or shifting a passband outside the target tolerance. We present a TMM-validated resonance-correction workflow for Ge/SiO2 Fabry-Perot distributed Bragg reflector (FP-DBR) filters operating from 3 to 5 μm. The planar stack is parameterized as Air / (Ge, SiO2)^N / SiO2 cavity / (Ge, SiO2)^N / substrate with design variables [dH,dL,N,Lc]. A teacher-replay conditional variational autoencoder generates multiple inverse candidates and a peak-aware surrogate provides prefiltering, but final selection is made by transfer matrix method (TMM) reranking and peak-valid validation. If the selected primary structure fails the peak-valid gate, a residual search around DBR quarter-wave and Fabry-Perot cavity relations is followed by local TMM refinement. On a unified 50-target benchmark with 60 thickness-perturbation trials per selected design, the workflow improved the nominal-valid rate from 70% to 92%, the robust-valid rate from 48% to 64%, and the mean perturbation yield from 0.471 to 0.558 relative to the baseline CVAE. Phase-residual analysis of a repaired target indicates that the fallback branch corrects the FP-DBR resonance condition rather than only reducing broadband error. These results support a peak-aware, TMM-validated route for inverse design of resonant multilayer filters within this one-dimensional FP-DBR family.

Keywords
Mid-infrared filter; FP-DBR; multilayer optics; transfer matrix method; inverse design; resonance correction
