# Changes Made Before Transfer

Dear Editor,

Following the rejection of the earlier submission and before transferring the manuscript to *Optics and Lasers in Engineering*, I revised the paper to better match the journal's emphasis on optical methods, engineering use, and practical validation. The manuscript is not submitted as an unchanged transfer.

The main changes are as follows:

1. The title, abstract, highlights, introduction, discussion, and conclusion were reframed around TMM-validated optical-filter design and engineering acceptance. The revised manuscript now emphasizes resonance-level peak matching, false-passband rejection, and thickness-perturbation reliability rather than a general machine-learning contribution.

2. The design problem was made more explicit for readers of an engineering optics journal. The manuscript now states the FP-DBR structure, the four design variables, the design bounds, the 3--5 um operating band, the TMM assumptions, and the intended in-family use case more directly.

3. The workflow description was revised to clarify the role of each module. Candidate generation and surrogate screening are used to reduce the search space, but final acceptance is based on TMM reranking, nominal peak-valid criteria, and thickness-perturbation validation.

4. The claims were tightened to avoid overgeneralization. The revised manuscript does not claim complete device fabrication, material-independent transfer, a PINN formulation, arbitrary 2D or 3D topology optimization, or a new general AI architecture.

5. The supporting evidence was kept visible and bounded. The manuscript retains the unified 50-target, 60-trial benchmark, direct TMM optimization sanity checks, threshold-sensitivity analysis, fallback accounting, and external-material sensitivity check, while explaining their scope and limitations.

These revisions were made to present the work as a practical optical-engineering design and validation workflow for mid-infrared Ge/SiO2 FP-DBR filters, which I believe is more appropriate for *Optics and Lasers in Engineering*.

Sincerely,

Chaoxu Liu
